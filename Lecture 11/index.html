Semantic Tags

Semantic tags are HTML elements that clearly describe their meaning in a human- and machine-readable way. They provide better structure to the document and improve accessibility, SEO, and maintainability.

Examples of Semantic Tags

  • <header>: Represents introductory content or a set of navigational links.
  • <nav>: Represents a section of a page that links to other pages or to parts within the page.
  • <main>: Represents the main content of the document.
  • <article>: Represents a self-contained composition in a document, such as a blog post or news article.
  • <section>: Represents a generic section of a document, typically with a heading.
  • <aside>: Represents content that is tangentially related to the content around it, such as sidebars or call-out boxes.
  • <footer>: Represents the footer for its nearest sectioning content or sectioning root element.

Using semantic tags helps improve the accessibility of your web pages, as screen readers and other assistive technologies can better understand the structure and content of the page. It also enhances SEO, as search engines can more accurately index and rank your content.
